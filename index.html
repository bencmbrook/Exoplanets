What are Exoplanets?

An exoplanet, or extrasolar planet, is a planet that orbits a star other than the Sun.

Astronomers discover them by looking for stars that wobble or dim periodically.

WOBBLING STAR

Within a solar system, planets orbit the host star due to the star's gravitational force on the planets. However, planets also exert gravitational forces on its star, causing the star to have a slight wobble. Scientists use small variations in a star's movement to detect the presence of planets that would otherwise be invisible.

DIMMING STAR

When a planet passes between a star and our telescopes, it blocks some of the star's light. For a brief period of time, that star actually gets dimmer. If this dimming is repetitive, it is likely caused by an orbiting planet.
